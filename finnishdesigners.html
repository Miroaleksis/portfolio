Problem

Customer provided us user research data from questionaires and interviews from which we started to define the problem with the current website and user personas based on most common user types and their goals.

Pre-existing pain points related to the search engine

  • Insufficient search options
  • Irrelevant search results
  • Many portfolios were not up to date

User personas

  • Junior: a newbie designer, usually straight out of school, main goals are finding a job and networking.
  • Veteran: an experienced designer who seeks to be part of community and affirm their status as a designer
  • Employer: Goal is to find a suitable worker quickly

Finnish Designers' competition advantage

Finnish Designers is a platfrom containing solely qualified designers because there is an application process for registering to the service, only designers with proper education or work experience can join. This makes it easy for employer to find suitable worker there compared to other services such as Oikopolku and Duunitori that are not focused on the design field and therefore lack proper knowledge of it. However, to make Finnishdesigners attractive to employers, browsing through the website and especially the search engine needs to work seamlessly.

Solution

After recognizing the pain points and making the user personas, we started to define the backbone of the user experience, design drivers, common user flows and goals. We made the user flows from point of view of the employer and the designer.

Design Drivers

  • Efficiency: fast and effortless to use
  • Sense of community and support: the platform helps and guides, interaction with other users
  • Personality: designer's personality and handprint can be easily seen from portfolio
  • Inspirational: user gets a perception of the design field and it's diversity

Vuorox

The task was to design a concept for Plaincomplex's Vuorox, an app where nurses can check and manage their shifts. The goal was not to build a finished product, but a concept model that the customer can utilize however they wish to further develop their app later. The project was carried out by a three-person team, including myself. I was part of every step of the process.

To build a user-friendly concept, we mapped out the end users' needs by conducting semi-structured interviews with nurses. From the interviews we discovered users' main priorities (maximum control over work shifts, simplicity in observing upcoming shifts) and the most common user types (diginatives, change-resistant senior employees and non-finnish speaking employees).

Based on the research we used user flows to map out the main use scenarios and determined design drivers for our app (perceivable at a glance, gives a sense of control, considers different needs, is trustworthy). After that we finally designed a prototype of the app with Figma. We used it to demonstrate the key functions: checking upcoming shifts, leaving shift and day off requests and managing general work time requests. All the UI elements were tested for accessibility.
